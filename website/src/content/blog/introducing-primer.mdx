When we first released Primer, it was an analytics dashboard. You could see how many sessions your team ran, what they cost, and where friction occurred. Useful — but fundamentally passive.

With v0.2, Primer becomes something different: an **intelligence platform** that answers specific questions at every level of your organization.

## The questions that matter

Engineering leaders have been asking us variants of the same three questions:

**"Is our AI investment paying off?"** Not just "are people using it" — but cost per successful outcome, ROI ratio, and whether the money would be better spent on training versus tooling.

**"What's actually blocking effectiveness?"** Not just "errors happen" — but which specific friction types correlate with the biggest drops in success rates, and which projects are hit hardest.

**"How do I help individual engineers improve?"** Not just leaderboards — but personalized trajectory analysis, skill gap detection, and learning paths generated from what works for engineers in similar roles.

## What's new

### Dedicated intelligence pages

The old 6-tab dashboard is gone. In its place: a hub-and-spoke design with dedicated pages for each insight area.

- **Friction Intelligence** — Trend charts, impact scoring by friction type, project-level breakdown. You can now see that `permission_denied` errors cause a 38 percentage point drop in success rate — and prioritize accordingly.
- **Code Quality** — Claude-assisted PRs vs. non-Claude, side by side. Merge rates, review comments, time to merge. The data to answer the skeptic's "is AI actually helping?"
- **Growth & Skills** — Onboarding cohort comparison, shared behavior patterns across your team, skill gap analysis. See how fast new hires ramp up versus the team baseline.
- **Session Insights** — Health distribution, satisfaction trends, goal analytics, end reason breakdown, permission mode analysis, cache efficiency. The aggregate view of what's working and what isn't.
- **Tool Adoption** — Which tools are being adopted, at what rate, and by whom. Now a tab on the AI Maturity page.

### Multi-agent hooks

Gemini CLI now has first-class SessionEnd hook support, just like Claude Code. Codex CLI users can use `primer sync --watch` for continuous collection. One platform, three agents.

### Intelligence, not just charts

Every metric in Primer now exists to answer a question. The new [What You Can Learn](/primer/docs/insights/) documentation explains what to measure, what healthy ranges look like, and what actions to take for each signal.

## Self-hosted, privacy-first

Nothing has changed here. Your session data — which contains your team's code, prompts, and workflow patterns — stays on your infrastructure. No telemetry, no cloud dependency.

## Get started

```bash
pip install primer
primer init
primer server start
primer hook install
```
