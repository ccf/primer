Most engineering leaders tracking AI tool adoption start with the wrong metric. They count sessions, or they count cost, and draw conclusions from one number. Here's a better framework.

## The metric that matters most: cost per successful outcome

Raw session count tells you adoption happened. Raw cost tells you money was spent. Neither tells you whether the investment is working.

**Cost per successful outcome** — total spend divided by sessions that actually achieved their goal — is the single metric that connects spend to value. If it's trending down, you're getting more efficient. If it's trending up, either costs are rising or success rates are falling, and you need to know which.

Primer computes this automatically. You'll find it on the FinOps page.

## Leading vs. lagging indicators

Most dashboards show lagging indicators: success rates, costs, PR merge rates. These confirm what already happened. To make decisions, you need leading indicators that predict where things are headed.

| Leading (act on these) | Lagging (validate with these) |
|------------------------|------------------------------|
| Adoption rate trend | Success rate |
| Tool diversity per engineer | Cost per outcome |
| Leverage score trajectory | PR merge rate |
| Friction type emergence | Session health distribution |

If your adoption rate is climbing but leverage scores are flat, engineers are using AI tools more but not getting more sophisticated about it. That's a training signal, not a success signal.

## Friction analysis: the insight most teams miss

Counting errors is noise. What matters is **which errors correlate with failure**.

Primer's friction impact scoring measures two things for each friction type: how often it occurs, and how much it reduces success rates. A friction type that happens rarely but tanks success by 40 percentage points is a higher priority fix than one that happens constantly but barely affects outcomes.

The Friction Intelligence page ranks these automatically. In our experience, the top three are usually:

1. **Permission blocks** — the AI tool tried to access something it couldn't. Fix: update your tool's allowed directories or permission settings.
2. **Context limits** — sessions hitting the token limit. Fix: break work into smaller, focused tasks and invest in project-scoped context files (CLAUDE.md).
3. **Tool errors** — MCP servers or integrations failing silently. Fix: audit your tooling setup with `primer doctor`.

## Adoption isn't binary

Teams often treat AI adoption as a yes/no question. In practice, there are distinct levels:

**Level 1: Occasional use.** Engineers use AI for quick questions. Low session count, basic tools (Read, Grep). Leverage score under 25.

**Level 2: Regular integration.** AI is part of daily workflow. 1-3 sessions per day, expanding tool usage. Leverage score 25-50.

**Level 3: Sophisticated orchestration.** Engineers use agents, skills, multi-step workflows, and prompt caching effectively. Leverage score above 50. These engineers typically have 3-6 sessions per day and high success rates.

Primer's AI Maturity page tracks this progression with a composite leverage score per engineer, plus tool category breakdowns and orchestration adoption rates. The Growth page shows how engineers move between levels over time.

## What to do with the data

The hardest part isn't collecting metrics — it's deciding what to do with them. Here's a decision framework:

**High adoption, low success rate?** Your engineers are trying but struggling. Invest in CLAUDE.md files, shared prompt libraries, and tool configuration guides. Look at the friction breakdown to identify specific blockers.

**Low adoption, high success rate?** A few people love it, most haven't tried. Run demos, pair programming sessions, and reduce setup friction. The Growth page's shared pattern analysis shows what your power users do differently.

**Rising costs, flat success?** Usage is growing but efficiency isn't improving. Check cache hit rates (are engineers reusing context effectively?) and session scope (are people trying to do too much in single sessions?).

**Flat leverage scores across the team?** Engineers have stopped exploring new capabilities. Share patterns from high-leverage users, run workshops on advanced features, and check whether your CLAUDE.md and project configuration files are keeping up with your tools.

## Healthy benchmarks

These are starting points, not hard targets. Every organization is different.

| Metric | Concerning | Healthy | Excellent |
|--------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Adoption rate | < 40% | 60-85% | > 85% |
| Success rate | < 45% | 55-70% | > 75% |
| Sessions/engineer/day | < 0.5 | 1-3 | 3-6 |
| Leverage score | < 25 | 35-55 | > 60 |
| Cache hit rate | < 20% | 30-50% | > 50% |
